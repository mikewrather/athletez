Welcome,

Athletez was built with you in mind but just in case we managed to confuse you we thought we should still give you some tips on how the whole thing works.

 

Vote

Click vote on anything on the Athletez site that you think is cool: photos, videos, players, games, or teams. The total number of votes something receives on the site determines where it shows up on the home page. In addition, the highest voted picture for every team and game will display as its default profile picture until something gets more votes.

Fan

Want to let the world know that you support an athlete or a team? Click follow.

Once you click follow your profile will display in the “Fan” section of whoever you followed, couldn’t think of an easier way to let the world know you support someone.

In addition, in a matter of weeks the follow button will also determine what populates an upcoming feature: your feed. The feed will chronicle and notify you of all activity that’s related to the people and teams you follow. It’ll be the best way to see when there are new photos, videos, comments, game scores and the like.

 

Tagging

As you’ve already noticed, we’ve got tons of awesome photo and video content here on Athletez. If you’ve made it this far, I’ll assume that you’re interested in sharing some of your ownâ€¦ and you want it to be seen by the right people.

On Athletez you can tag Games, Teams, Athletes, Events or some combination of the four. The photo or video tagged will then display in the media section of the respective pages.

A few notes on tagging:

- A photo or video will display in the media section of all pages that have been tagged
- Photos and videos will automatically be tagged with whatever game, team, or player page you select upload from.
- If you tag a game, both teams that played will automatically be tagged.
- All home page search is a result of tagging, so if you don’t properly tag your media it won’t show up where you want it to!

 

 

Connecting with teams

Connect yourself with every team you’ve ever played on, in seconds!

Once you’ve added yourself to a team on your Teams and Sports page the following things will happen:

1.    A new view of your profile will be available for the sport of the team whose roster you’ve added yourself to. This is where your photos, videos, game schedules and workout stats will appear on your profile.
2.    Your profile will begin to display in search results for the new sport.
3.    The game schedule and results for that team will display on your profile page.
4.    You will be added to the roster section of that Team page, and any game page associated with that team.
5.    You will receive email notifications if when anyone tags your team in any media content, or comments on any games your team played in.

Connecting with Individual Sports & Events

Simply put, individual sports are those that you train for and compete in without necessarily having to belong to a Club, School or Specific gym.

Long distance runners, rock-climbers, triathletes and golfers are the easiest examples of this type of connection.

The key difference between connecting your profile with an individual sport as opposed to a team sport is the ability to link your profile with Events. Events are to individual sports as Games are to Team Sports.

Examples of events include beach volleyball tournaments, marathons, triathlons and the like. You can either create an event from your profile page (when the correct individual sport has been selected) or add yourself as a participant from an existing event page.

Creating a Game

We did our best to make creating a game, and updating your team schedules as easy as possible. You can create a game from the Schedule Section of any profile as well as any Team page.

After that it’s as simple as selecting the two teams that competed, entering the game score (if the event already took place), uploading some photos and videos and sharing the link with your friends and fans.

You can edit the game score, date, time, or address from the game page itself after it has been created.

Creating an Event

As mentioned in the “Individual Sports & Events” section above, events are the key component of Individual Sports.

Creating an Event is as simple as selecting the “Create Event” button in the event schedule of any athlete’s profile. After that you need only assign a sport, event name, date, time, and location and click create!

If it turns out someone has already added the Event you just need to add yourself as a participant from the event page and then you will display as a participant from the event page, receive notifications regarding the event, and have the event added to your event schedule for that sport.
